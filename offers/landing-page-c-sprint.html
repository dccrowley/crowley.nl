The Situation

Your leadership team is not aligned. That is the real blocker.

  • Different leaders have different views on AI. Some are enthusiastic. Some are sceptical. Nobody is sure who is right.
  • People across the business are experimenting individually. There is no shared direction and no way to tell what is working.
  • You have had the AI conversation three times already. It ends the same way: "We should do something." Then nothing happens.
  • You need the entire leadership team in the room, making real decisions, with a plan they all own when they walk out.
How the Sprint Works

Three phases. One clear outcome.

The sprint is designed to move fast and produce something you can act on immediately. No theory. No slides. Just decisions.

Pre-work1-2 weeks before
Assessment and preparation
Each leader completes a short AI readiness questionnaire. I review your tech stack, team structure, and current AI usage. I interview 2-3 key people to understand the real situation. You arrive on Day 1 with a clear picture of where you stand.
Day 1Full day with leadership team
Audit and prioritise
Morning: we walk through the assessment results together. Every leader sees the same picture for the first time. We map AI usage across the business and name the real blockers. Afternoon: we score your top workflows for AI potential. By the end of the day, the team has agreed on 2-3 areas to focus on first.
Day 2Full day with leadership team
Plan and commit
Morning: I demonstrate what AI-assisted work looks like in your chosen focus areas, using your actual business context. Afternoon: we build the 30-day pilot plan together. Who does what. How you will measure it. What success looks like. Every leader leaves with a clear role and a specific commitment.
Follow-up30 days after
Review and adjust
One month later, we reconvene for a 90-minute session. We review the pilot results, fix what needs fixing, and build the 90-day roadmap for the next phase of adoption. This session is included in the sprint price.
