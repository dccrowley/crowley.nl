Selected Work

Four projects that show how I think about design leadership: finding the real problem, building the right team, and making the outcome stick.

01

Strategic Discovery for a 2M+ User Platform

Iddink Group (Sanoma Learning) · Netherlands · 2018–2022

Magister held 80% of the Dutch secondary education market. By the time I arrived, that had dropped to 60% and the 14-year-old codebase had no modernisation path. I led a top-task research programme that revealed 95% of daily usage concentrated in just 6–10 tasks. That finding became the foundation of a phased business case I presented to the board of directors.

Outcome: Board approved a three-year roadmap after three years of inaction. €3M+ development investment secured. Market share stabilised.
What mattered: Six interviews unlocked more investment than years of feature requests. The board needed evidence, not enthusiasm.

02

Building AI Capability Across 40 People

Alef Education · 2023–present

The organisation had no AI literacy and no integration strategy. I built a training programme from scratch: a five-level prompting framework, hands-on workshops for the international team, research synthesis workflows, and automation tooling. The programme changed how teams make decisions, not just which tools they use.

Outcome: 40+ colleagues trained. Research synthesis reduced from days to hours. Manual workflows cut by over 60%.
What mattered: Teaching people to think with AI at the start of a task, not reach for it as a last resort.

03

Design System & Product Operations

Alef Education · 2022–present

Sixty-plus templates with no governance. Product decisions made without structured user feedback. I consolidated the design system to 22 reusable components and built a feedback analytics dashboard connecting Usersnap qualitative data to Amplitude quantitative patterns. Two sides of the same problem: building the infrastructure that lets a design org operate at scale.

Outcome: £200K annual savings. 40% faster production. Decision-making reduced from 10 days to 3.
What mattered: The discipline to cut 60 templates to 22. And connecting the data nobody was connecting.

04

Building a Cross-Cultural Design Organisation

Alef Education · 2022–present

Sixteen people across six nationalities and twelve markets. This is not a project case study. It is the story of building a team where critique is normal, growth is expected, and people choose to stay. I invested in structured certifications, adapted communication for cultural context, and built the rituals that create psychological safety through predictability.

Outcome: Zero attrition over 18 months. Multiple junior-to-mid promotions. Design earned a seat at the product table.
What mattered: People stay where they are growing. That is the retention strategy.

How I Work

I trained in the tradition of the Swiss International Typographic Style and Spiekermann’s typographic principles. That foundation shapes how I think about every system I build: clarity is a functional requirement, not a stylistic preference.

Systems over surfaces

I build design systems, operational processes, and team structures that outlast any single project or person. When I leave an organisation, things should be easier to run than when I arrived. That is the measure.

Clarity enables autonomy

I lead multicultural teams by setting clear goals and creating genuine space for critique. My current team spans eight nationalities. They do not need close management. They need context, honest feedback, and trust that their decisions will be respected.

Evidence before opinion

I present to boards with research, not concept decks. When a product needed a new direction, I started with what users actually did. Opinion earns its seat at the table; it does not automatically occupy it.

AI as an organisational capability

I have integrated AI into design workflows, built training programmes for cross-functional teams, and seen what changes when people learn to think with AI rather than simply deploy it. The value is not in the tool. It is in the decision-making shift that follows.
